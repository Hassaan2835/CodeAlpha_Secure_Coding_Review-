Understanding Social Engineering & Phishing

Phishing is a form of social engineering where attackers impersonate trustworthy entities to deceive victims into revealing sensitive information, clicking malicious links, or downloading viruses.

Core Phishing Vectors
  • Email Phishing: Bulk emails mimicking corporations (like Netflix, banks, or DHL) containing bad URLs or attachments.
  • Spear Phishing: Highly personalized phishing targets aiming at specific individuals using custom details.
  • Whaling: Spear phishing directed specifically at high-profile executives (CEOs, CFOs) to steal major company funds or assets.
  • Smishing (SMS Phishing): Attacks conducted via text messages, often containing fake package deliveries or bank alert links.
  • Vishing (Voice Phishing): Fraudulent phone calls (often spoofing real numbers) using voice deception or AI-voice synthesis to gather details.
Social Engineering Psychological Triggers

Attackers exploit human psychology instead of system code. They rely on key triggers:

Urgency: "Act within 24 hours or lose access." Prevents critical analysis.
Fear/Intimidation: "Legal action will be taken against you." Triggers panic.
Authority: "Request from the CEO / IT Department." Inhibits questioning requests.
Greed/Reward: "You won a $500 Amazon Gift card! Click here." Exploits optimism.
Curiosity: "Check out these leaked photos!" Hooks natural inquisitiveness.

Spotting Deceptive & Spoofed Websites

Cybercriminals design landing pages that look identical to authentic websites (like Microsoft, Google, or Chase Bank). However, they cannot forge official domain names and security certifications.

How to check:

  1. Check the Domain: Make sure the exact domain matches. Look for lookalikes (typosquatting) like paypal-update.com instead of paypal.com, or character substitutions like arnazon.com (r+n mimics m).
  2. Check SSL/HTTPS: Secure sites use https:// with a padlock. Phishing sites may use standard http:// or procure free SSL certs for fake domains.
  3. Check Subdomain Structure: In a URL, the true domain sits immediately before the first single slash (excluding path folders). For example: netflix.security-login.com is hosted on security-login.com, NOT Netflix.
